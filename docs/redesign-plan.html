SimpleFOCMini — 60 V Redesign Plan

Working plan for reworking the SimpleFOCMini (TI DRV8313PWPR) from its v1.1 8–24 V rating to the DRV8313's full voltage range. Derived from the audit of the imported KiCad project (KiCad/project/).

Implementation status (updated 2026-06-17). The analysis below is the original plan. The schematic changes (cap values/ratings, per-symbol MPNs, the C5 parallel bulk cap, LED re-sited to 3.3 V) and the PCB thermal core (U1 EP thermal vias netted to GND, two inner GND planes, VM trace widened to 0.8 mm) are applied, and every "verify/decide/open" item below is resolved. Live per-part status and the remaining re-layout work are tracked in redesign-bom.md.

The datasheet's "2.5 A per phase" is a peak figure; TI publishes no continuous/RMS rating (it is thermally limited). 1.5 A continuous is the thermally realistic rating on a small 1 oz board; the thermal design targets ~2 A of headroom.

Continuous current capability (1 oz copper)

The continuous limit is set by the DRV8313 junction temperature, not the copper — even 2 A needs only a 0.78 mm trace on 1 oz (10 °C rise), and GND-plane pours make trace ampacity a non-issue. The binding variable is RθJA. Conduction loss is P = 3 · I² · RDS(on); with a ~25 % switching allowance at 60 V and Tj ≤ 125 °C (reliability margin), 25 °C ambient:

RθJAContinuous A/phaseNotes
25 °C/W~1.8 Abest case — large copper, cool/airflow
30 °C/W~1.6 Agood 4-layer with EP via array
35 °C/W~1.5 Arealistic for a small still-air board
40 °C/W~1.4 Aconservative

A board ≤50 mm in still air realistically sits at RθJA ≈ 30–40 °C/W even with a good via array (small boards have limited area to shed heat to air). 1.5 A continuous is therefore "free" relative to the 1.2 A baseline — same parts, 1 oz copper, just the planned thermal-via array + pours. ~1.75–2 A is reachable with maximal copper / cooler ambient / light airflow but with thin margin; beyond ~2 A needs 2 oz copper or forced air. Validate on the bench before rating higher.

Layout / stackup requirements

  • 4-layer Top / GND / GND / Bottom. Outer layers carry signal + power copper; inner layers are solid GND for thermal spreading and as the return plane for the 60 V switching nodes.
  • Thermal vias under U1's exposed pad (EP) — applied. The footprint already contained an 8-pad PTH thermal-via array under the EP, but the import left it unnetted (floating) — it is now netted to GND, and two inner GND planes were added. (The earlier "zero vias" audit reading was that miss.) This is the single most important thermal fix.
  • Trace widths (1 oz): ≥0.53 mm meets a 10 °C rise at 1.5 A; prefer distributing VM/phase current through pours/planes. The VM trace is now 0.8 mm (was 0.254 mm). Phase traces stay 0.5 mm at the HTSSOP escape — 0.8 mm shorts adjacent 0.65 mm-pitch pins, so neck/widen them during routing.
  • Clearance for 60 V: current min is 0.20 mm (passes IPC-2221 but thin). Open power-net clearance to ≥0.25–0.3 mm.
  • Bulk cap close to U1's VM pins; wide/short high-current path, multiple vias (datasheet §10.1).
  • Maximize copper area around U1 and on the GND planes — this directly lowers RθJA and is what buys the headroom toward 2 A.

Thermal budget (1.5 A, sanity check)

Conduction loss ≈ 3 · I² · RDS(on) ≈ 2.2 W (hot); ~2.5–2.7 W including switching at 60 V. With EP vias + GND planes (RθJA ≈ 30–35 °C/W), ΔTj ≈ 80–95 °C → Tj ≈ 105–120 °C at 25 °C ambient — within the 125 °C reliability target, but to be confirmed on the bench. Headroom shrinks at higher ambient.

Open items

  • R5 value — resolved: kept at 1 kΩ (≈1.3 mA on the 3.3 V rail). No physical board was available to settle the 1 kΩ-vs-4.7 kΩ part-number discrepancy; 1 kΩ was chosen as correct for a 3.3 V indicator.
  • C1 value — resolved: 10 nF (the DRV8313 datasheet value), 100 V X7R.
  • Charge-pump cap connectivity (C1 across CP1/CP2; C2 across VCP/VM) — verify in the final layout.
  • Bench-validate the thermal performance before rating continuous current above ~1.5 A.
